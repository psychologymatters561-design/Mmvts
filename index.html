Dog Vomiting in Delhi: Causes, Home Care & Vet Guidance

Overview: Vomiting is different from regurgitation. Common preceding signs include nausea, drooling, and retching. If your dog is vomiting in East Delhi or anywhere in the NCR, knowing the difference can save their life.

Symptoms & Red Flags

  • Bloody vomit or repeated episodes.
  • Dehydration signs: dry gums, sunken eyes.
  • Weakness/Lethargy: If vomiting lasts >24 hrs or your pet seems weak, consult a vet immediately.

Common Causes

Gastroenteritis (parvovirus, food poisoning, dietary indiscretion), parasites, heatstroke, kidney or liver disease. Vomiting can be caused by many disorders including digestive disease, kidney failure, pancreatitis, or poisons.

Home First Aid (kutta ulti kya karein)

Stop food for 12–24h (offer ice chips for hydration). Use unflavored Pedialyte or boiled rice water to replenish electrolytes. Introduce small bland meals (boiled rice/chicken) when vomiting stops.

When to Visit the Vet

Continual vomiting >1–2 days, blood, signs of pain, or if it's a puppy/senior dog. If vomiting is accompanied by blood, pain, or depression, it requires a prompt exam.

Diarrhea in Dogs & Cats – Home Care & When to See a Vet

Diarrhea (loose stool) is common. Look for straining, mucus, or blood in feces. Cats and dogs with diarrhea may also vomit or be lethargic. Colitis causes mucus/blood and small volume frequent stools.

Causes & Local Risks

Parasites (Giardia, worms), dietary changes, infections, toxins, pancreatitis, colitis. Local Tip: Avoid giving pets contaminated municipal water – use filtered water. In Delhi’s monsoon, be wary of leptospirosis and ensure vaccinations are up to date.

Home Care for Loose Motions

Fast your pet for 12–24h, give water/safe fluids (PED, rice water). Feed a bland diet (boiled rice & chicken). You can use probiotics or canned pumpkin (fiber). Monitor hydration closely.

Veterinary Care & Treatment

If it persists >24h, there's fever/depression, the pet won’t drink, or the pet is young/old, see a vet. Diagnostics may include fecal float, snap tests, bloodwork, and imaging. Treatment involves rehydration (Oral VetLyte or IV fluids), antibiotics, or deworming as needed.

Lethargy & Weakness in Dogs & Cats – Don’t Ignore These Signs

Lethargy means the pet is unusually tired or inactive. Often owners notice the pet sleeping more or showing a reluctance to play. While it might seem minor, it's a broad indicator of underlying issues.

Potential Causes

Systemic illness (infections, organ failure, cancer), pain (arthritis), anemia, or even emotional distress. In older pets, it's often dismissed as "just aging," but it warrants investigation.
